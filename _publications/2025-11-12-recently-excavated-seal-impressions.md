**Abstract:** Seal impressions at Middle Kingdom sites can be informative on the nature and significance of the administrative activity of their inhabitants. The present article analyzes a corpus of 372 seal impressions from a Middle Kingdom settlement site on Elephantine Island, excavated by the German Archaeological Institute project “Realities of Life.” I will use the typology established by Olga Tufnell and William Ward to classify the decorative and textual contents of the impressions. I will also identify “back types,” which inform what types of containers were sealed. I will be able to compare the Elephantine corpus with those found at contemporary sites for additional context. Finally, I will discuss the significance of a few specific seal impressions which hold noteworthy names and titles. This analysis will serve to clarify the meaning, function, and context of seal impressions found at Elephantine.
