Welcome to my website! I am an Egyptian and Nubian archaeologist currently pursuing a Ph.D. at UC Santa Barbara. I study daily life in colonial communities in Lower Nubia during the second and first millennium BCE, with a focus on indigenous Nubian expressions of identity and resistance, as well as gender and familial ties. My expertise is in seals and sealing practices during the Middle Kingdom and Second Intermediate Period, but I am pivoting to broader small finds analysis. I hold a M.A. in Art History, with a concentration in Egyptian Art and Archaeology from the University of Memphis and a B.A. in History and Latin from Creighton University. 

A Better World is Possible
======
As an American academic and an archaeologist, I recognize that I sit in a position that grants me an enormous amount of privilege and power, and that both my positionality and my field are fundamentally tied to and benefit from American (and previously, Western European) hegemony, which rests on foundations of white supremacy, patriarchy, and capitalism. The world we live in now, the conditions under which we find ourselves, are not absolute, and it is imperative that we take it upon ourselves to believe in, discuss, and most importantly, act on the knowledge that a better world is possible, and we can build it. 

With this in mind, I would ask you, especially if you enjoy and/or use my work, to donate to one or more of the following fundraisers: [https://linktr.ee/kat_consola](https://linktr.ee/kat_consola). It is far from the *only* thing we can or should be doing towards that better world, but it is one thing we can do. 

Teaching Materials
======
Prior to beginning my Ph.D. studies at UCSB, I taught World History and Latin at a middle school in Memphis, Tennessee for a few years. It is my intention to eventually upload many or all of my teaching materials for other teachers to use, as finding reliable resources as a new teacher can often be quite challenging.
